About

I’m an undergraduate computer science major at the University of Massachusetts Boston with a growing interest in machine learning, artificial intelligence, and their applications in healthcare. I’m drawn to problems where data is limited, uncertainty is high, and model reliability truly matters — especially rare disease detection and clinical decision support.

I’m also interested in trustworthy AI, particularly how we can design systems that clinicians and patients can understand and rely on.

Research Project

Hallucination Detection with Fine‑Tuned Language Models

PyTorch · HuggingFace · LoRA · GPT‑2

Fine‑tuned a GPT‑2‑based sequence classification model using LoRA to detect hallucinated vs. factual responses in dialogue data. Built a binary dataset from the HaluEval benchmark, trained the model using the HuggingFace Trainer, and evaluated improvements over the base model. This project strengthened my understanding of model reliability and PEFT methods for efficient fine‑tuning.

Research Direction

LLM Benchmarking for Rare Disease Diagnosis

GPT‑4o · GitHub Models · AI × Healthcare

Designed an evaluation framework to test whether LLM diagnostic accuracy changes across disease rarity tiers. This work explores how LLMs reason about rare conditions, where data scarcity and clinical ambiguity make diagnosis challenging. The goal is to understand model limitations and identify opportunities for safer, more reliable AI‑assisted clinical reasoning.

Research Project

EEG Data Analysis

Python · Data Visualization

Analyzed EEG datasets as part of a professor‑guided project. Cleaned and processed raw brain‑signal data, visualized patterns, and explored challenges in biomedical signal interpretation. This experience strengthened my analytical skills and exposed me to the complexity of real‑world healthcare data.
